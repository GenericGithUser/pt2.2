ability to express one's thoughts and emotions by speech sounds and gesture


>ðŸ“™Types of Speech

  • direct speech
  • indirect speech

>ðŸ“—Direct speech

Direct speech repeats, or quotes, the exact words spoken. When we use direct speech in writing, we place the words spoken between quotation marks (" ") and there is no change in these words.

Example: She says, "What time will you be home?"
Example: She said, "What time will you be home?" and I said, "I don't know! "
Example: "There's a fly in my soup!" screamed Simone.
Example: John said, "There's an elephant outside the window."

>ðŸ“• Indirect speech

Reported or indirect speech is usually used to talk about the past, so we normally change the tense of the words spoken. We use reporting verbs like 'say', 'tell', 'ask', and we may use the word 'that' to introduce the reported words.

Example: She told him she was happy.
Example: She said that she had seen him.
Example: He told me that he was tired.
Example: She was speaking on the telephone.

>ðŸ“—Tense changes when using reported speech

Normally, the tense in reported speech is one tense back in time from the tense in direct speech:

Example: "I always drink coffee", she said --> She said that she always drank coffee.
Example: "Bill arrived on Saturday", he said. --> He said that Bill had arrived on Saturday.
Example: "I am reading a book", he explained. --> He explained that he was reading a book
Example: "I have been to Spain", he told me. --> He told me that he had been to Spain.

>ðŸ“˜Changing time and place references when reporting speech

Time and place must often change when going from direct to reported speech.

Example: ""I saw him today", she said. --> She said that she had seen him that day.
Example: "I saw him yesterday", she said.--> She said that she had seen him the day before.
Example: "I met her the day before yesterday", he said. --> He said that he had met her two days before.
Example: "I'll see you tomorrow", he said --> He said that he would see me the next day.

>ðŸ“—Question forms and reporting speech

Word order:

Normal word order is used in reported questions, that is, the subject comes before the verb, and it is not necessary to use 'do' or 'did'.

Example: (direct to indirect) "Where does Peter live?" --> She asked him where Peter lived.
Example: (direct to indirect) "Where are you going?" --> She asked where I was going.

Yes / no questions

type of question is reported by using 'ask' + 'if / whether' + clause:

Example: (direct to indirect) "Do you speak English?" --> He asked me if I spoke English.
Example: (direct to indirect) "Are you British or American?"--> He asked me whether I was British or American.
example: (direct to indirect) "Is it raining?" --> She asked if it was raining.

Question words:

This type of question is reported by using 'ask' (or another verb like 'ask') + question word + clause. The clause contains the question, in normal word order and with the necessary tense change.

Example: (direct to indirect) "What is your name?" he asked me. --> He asked me what my name was.
Example: (direct to indirect) "How old is your mother?", he asked. --> He asked how old her mother was.
Example: (direct to indirect) "Why did you leave?", she asked. --> She asked why I had left.

>ðŸ“˜Reporting verbs

Reporting verbs are used to report what someone said or did. They are followed by the person who said or did it, and then the clause that contains the reported speech.Some reporting verbs may appear in more than one of the following groups because they can be used in several ways.

Verbs followed by "if" or "whether":

  • ask
  • know
  • remember
  • say
  • see

Common Verbs followed by a "that":

  • add
  • doubt
  • reply
  • admit
  • estimate
  • report
  • agree
  • explain
  • reveal

>ðŸ“˜Reporting orders, requests, and suggestions

When we want to report an order or request, we can use a verb like 'tell' with a to-clause: He told me to go away. The pattern is verb + indirect object + to-clause. The indirect object is the person spoken to. Other verbs used to report orders and requests in this way are: command, order, warn, ask, advise, invite, beg, teach, & forbid.

Example: (direct to indirect) The doctor said to me, "Stop smoking!". --> The doctor told me to stop smoking.
Example: (direct to indirect) "Get out of the car!" said the policeman. --> The policeman ordered him to get out of the car.
Example: (direct to indirect) "Could you please be quiet," she said. --> She asked me to be quiet.
Example: (direct to indirect) "Can I have an apple?", she asked. --> She asked for an apple.
Example: (direct to indirect) "Could I have three kilos of onions?" -->He asked for three kilos of onions.
