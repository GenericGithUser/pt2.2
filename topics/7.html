>ðŸ“˜ What are punctuatuations?

Punctuation is defined as a set of symbols used to separate and clarify the meaning of sentences and written elements. In other words, punctuation tells readers of your writing where to pause, what words are quotations and which are clarifications, where words have been omitted, and more.

>ðŸ“™ Types of punctuations

There are 6 types of punctuations namely:

  • Full stop
  • Comma
  • Colon
  • Semicolon
  • Question mark
  • Exclamation mark
  • Quotation marks
  • Hyphen

>ðŸ“• Full stop

A full stop is a punctuation mark that is used at the end of a sentence. It is also called a period. A full stop is used to mark the end of a sentence.

Example: We walked to the mall together.
Example: I jumped into the pool right after they signalled the start of the competition.
Example: I cleaned my bedroom because my parents told me to.

>ðŸ“™ Comma

Commas are used to separate the items in a list. They are also used to separate the elements of a compound sentence. For example: I went to the market, bought some vegetables and came back.

Example: I, the leader of the group, had trouble with my members
Example: I bought apples, oranges, tofu, and a cup of rice.
Example: I jumped off of the cliff, unaware of the rocky landing bellow, i was injured critically.

>ðŸ“• Colon

A colon is used to introduce a list or to introduce a quotation. For example: The following are the fruits available in the market: banana, apple, orange, etc.

Example: I use the following programming languages daily: Python, cobol , ruby, and C++
Example: I bought the following ingredients: Flour, milk and eggs
Example: My bedroom has the following items: a bed, a chair, a computer and a rug

>ðŸ“— Semicolon

A semicolon is used to separate two independent clauses that are closely related. For example: I went to the market; I bought some vegetables.

Example: I went to the mall; I bought a shirt, a pair of shoes, and a hat.
Example: I have a lot of things to do: I need to clean my room, I have to do the laundry, and I need to cook dinner.
Example: He said he had to go to the store; he was out of milk.

>ðŸ“™ Question mark

A question mark is used to indicate a question. For example: What is your name?

Example: What is the meaning of life?
Example: Where is the nearest rest room?
Example: Why is my bedroom so messy?

>ðŸ“™ Exclamation mark

An exclamation mark is used to indicate an exclamation. For example: I am very happy!

Example: Who did this?!
Example: Why are you so noisy?!
Example: Are you ok?!

>ðŸ“™ Quotation marks

Quotation marks are used to enclose a quotation.

Example: "i wanted to go to the concert" mark exclaimed.
Example: Zoe ran while shouting the words "help me!".
Example: billy shouted "finally" as he fixed a crucial bug in his program.

>ðŸ“™ Hyphen

Hyphens are used to join two or more words.

Example: we're looking for a cat-friendly apartment
Example: we live in a euro-centric country
Example: My house was built in the twentieth-century.
